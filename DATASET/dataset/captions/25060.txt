Land-based sports betting has been legal in the U.S. state of Nevada since 1949 . When the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act ( PASPA ) was passed by Congress in 1992 , Nevada was handed a legal monopoly on single-game wagering in the United States . Meaning that for decades it was possible to play college sports betting , MLB sports betting , NBA sports betting , and live and off-track horse betting in the state . Nowadays , sports betting has also been legalized in other U.S. states , however , Nevada still accounts for a sizable portion of the nationwide sports betting revenue . In August 2020 , Nevada had a sports betting handle of 474.95 million U.S. dollars , up from the previous month 's total of 165.55 million U.S. dollars .
