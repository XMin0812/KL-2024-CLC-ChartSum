This statistic presents data on the degree of inequality in wealth distribution based on the Gini coefficient in Guatemala between 2000 and 2017 . The Gini coefficient measures the deviation of the distribution of income ( or consumption ) among individuals or households in a given country from a perfectly equal distribution . A value of 0 represents absolute equality , whereas 100 would be the highest possible degree of inequality . As of 2017 , Guatemala had a Gini coefficient of 48.3 , an improvement from 55.9 as of 2012 .
