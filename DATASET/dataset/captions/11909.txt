In 2019 , the revolving credit outstanding in the United States amounted to 1.08 trillion U.S. dollars . Revolving credit is a type of credit that does not have a fixed number of payments , in contrast to an installment credit . The most common examples of revolving credit used by consumers are credit cards , although some credit cards are non-revolving , i.e . require users to pay off the full balance at the end of each month .
