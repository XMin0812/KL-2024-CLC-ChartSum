This statistic shows the percentage change on previous year of real disposable income of households in the Netherlands from the first quarter of 2016 to the first quarter of 2020 . Disposable income or disposable personal income ( DPI ) is the amount of money households have available for spending and saving after income taxes have accounted for . It is seen as one of many key economic indicators to show the overall health of an economy . As a concept , disposable income is deemed closer to the idea of income been as generally understood in economics , than is either national income or gross domestic product ( GDP ) . In the first quarter of 2020 , the real disposable income of households increased with 2.2 percent compared to the same quarter a year before . In 2019 , the saving rate of households is forecasted to increase by approximately 15 percent when compared to 2018 .
