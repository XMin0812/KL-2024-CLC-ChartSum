This statistic shows the most important factors for a European migrant to be accepted into British society , according to the respondents from an Ipsos MORI survey in Great Britain in December 2013 . 69 percent of respondents felt that learning the English language was the most important thing to do , followed by three economical factors : getting a job and paying taxes ( just under two thirds ) , not claiming any benefits ( just under a half ) and not sending remittances back home ( just under 30 percent ) .
