Load factor of combined heat and power ( CHP ) schemes in the United Kingdom ( UK ) presented a trend of decline despite some oscillation . Load factors are the ratio of how much electricity was produced as a share of the total generating capacity . Between 2000 and 2013 , load factor of cogeneration schemes decreased from the peak to the lowest within the period of consideration , from 64.7 percent to 37.8 percent , respectively . On the following years , load factor of CHP increased and amounted to 44.3 percent as of 2019 .
