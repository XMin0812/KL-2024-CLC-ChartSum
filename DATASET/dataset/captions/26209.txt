As of 2018 , the number of South African households living in formal dwelling has reached more than 80 percent , with the highest rates being observed in Limpopo ( 93 percent ) , Mpumalanga ( 87.7 percent ) , and Northern Cape ( 87.3 percent ) . 13.1 percent of South Africans , however , still inhabited informal dwellings , while the remaining five percent were living in traditional dwellings .
