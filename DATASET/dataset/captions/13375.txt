Capital ratios express a bank 's capital as a percentage of its risk-weighted assets ( RWAs ) . Capital requirements for European banks were raised after the Basel III accord and phased in on the 1st of January 2015 , with a new minimum requirement of CET1 ratio of 4.5 percent . As of the fourth quarter 2019 , the Finland headquartered bank OP Financial Group had the highest common equity tier 1 ratio of Europe 's largest banks with 19.5 percent .
