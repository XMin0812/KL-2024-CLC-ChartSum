In the first quarter of 2020 , the gross disposable income of Dutch households was 95 billion euros . In 2020 , the saving rate of households is forecasted to reach approximately 15 percent . Disposable income or disposable personal income ( DPI ) is the amount of money households have available for spending and saving after income taxes have been accounted for . It is seen as one of many key economic indicators to show the overall health of an economy . As a concept , disposable income is deemed closer to the idea of income as generally understood in economics , than is either national income or gross domestic product ( GDP ) .
