As of March 2020 , it is estimated that the total revenue loss of the primary agriculture will amount to five billion euros over a period of six months due to the coronavirus outbreak in the Netherlands . Horticulture foresee significant losses of two billion euros of revenue , whereas farms with ancillary activities expect approximately a decline of 200 million euros in total . When looking at the economic impact , Rabobank economists calculated that the growth of the Dutch economy will face a recession in 2020 due to the coronavirus and geopolitical events .
