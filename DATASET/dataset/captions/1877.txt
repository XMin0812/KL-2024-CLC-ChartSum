In 2019 , the Nigerian states of Sokoto and Taraba had the largest percentage of people living below the poverty line . The lowest poverty rates were recorded in the South and South-Western states . In Lagos , this figure equaled to 4.5 percent , the lowest rate in Nigeria . An individual is considered poor in Nigeria when has an availability of less than 137.4 thousand Nigerian Naira ( roughly 361 U.S. dollars ) per year . In total , 40.1 percent of population in Nigeria lived in poverty .
