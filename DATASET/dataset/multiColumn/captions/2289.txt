According to the latest governmental data from 2019 , the Gini coefficient in South Africa was 0.65 points in 2015 , with lesser inequality in income within the rural areas of the most southern country of Africa . The Gini index gives information on the distribution of income in a country . In an ideal situation in which incomes are perfectly distributed , the coefficient is equal to zero , whereas one represents the highest inequality situation . South Africa had the world 's highest inequality in income distribution . Furthermore , the first eight countries on the ranking are located in Sub-Saharan Africa , with an index over 50 points .
