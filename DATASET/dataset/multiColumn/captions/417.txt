Before the banknotes enter circulation , they must pass the production tests , related to the quality of notes and security of the money . Many factors influence the length of time a banknote stays in circulation , including the overall durability , but also non-altered signals in automated authentication , for security . The lifespan of notes can also vary for different denominations , as some are more frequently used in making payments . If a banknote no longer meets quality standards , it needs to be taken out of the market .
