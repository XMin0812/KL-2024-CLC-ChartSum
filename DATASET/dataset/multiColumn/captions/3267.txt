In the United States , natural gas prices were the highest in the residential sector at 10.6 U.S. dollars on average per 1,000 cubic feet . Commercial natural gas costs were second-highest , while prices in the industrial and electric power sector were the lowest , at less than five U.S. dollars on average . Prices for the industrial and electric power customers tend to be close to the wholesale electricity price as they tend to use more electricity and thus , supplying electricity to the customers is more efficient and less expensive .
