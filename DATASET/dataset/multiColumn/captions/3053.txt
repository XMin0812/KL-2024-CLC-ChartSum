In 2017 , the occupancy rate of curative ( acute ) care beds in hospitals in Ireland was almost 95 percent . In the year 2000 , Ireland had an acute care bed occupancy rate of around 85 percent . High occupancy rates may be a sign that a health system is under pressure and that there may be a risk of bed shortages and higher rates of infection . This statistic shows the occupancy rates of acute care beds in hospitals in select countries worldwide in 2000 and 2017 .
