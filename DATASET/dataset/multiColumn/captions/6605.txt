In 2018 , the at-risk-of-poverty rate was one percentage higher for women than for men in Finland . The share of women below the poverty risk threshold was 12.3 percent , compared to 11.3 percent of males . The poverty risk rate peaked in 2008 at 14.8 percent for women and 12.9 percent for men , but dropped thereafter below 12 percent by 2015 .
