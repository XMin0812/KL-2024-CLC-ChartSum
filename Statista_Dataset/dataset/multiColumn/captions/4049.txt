In 2019 , the travel and tourism sector accounted for 13.9 percent of the gross domestic product in the Caribbean , directly and indirectly . Nevertheless , the majority of Caribbean islands registered higher shares of tourism contribution to their GDPs that year . For instance , the travel and tourism sector directly represented around one third of Aruba 's GDP and accounted for nearly 74 percent of the island 's GDP in total . The Former Netherlands Antilles as well as the British and the U.S. Virgin Islands also registered shares over 55 percent of total tourism contribution to GDP that year .
