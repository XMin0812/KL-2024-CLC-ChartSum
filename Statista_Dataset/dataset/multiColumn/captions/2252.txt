The outbreak of the coronavirus ( COVID-19 ) pandemic has changed the way smartphone manufacturers and vendors have been able to conduct their business , with shutdowns and disruptions all along the supply chain . On the user side , challenging economic conditions and employment insecurity has also led to consumers deferring their purchase of expensive items such as smartphones . While over 1.4 billion units had been expected to be shipped in 2020 before the pandemic , the corresponding number from the revised forecast is now roughly 1.3 billion devices .
