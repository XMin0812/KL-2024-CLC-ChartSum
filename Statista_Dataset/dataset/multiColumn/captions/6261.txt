In 2019 , about 64 percent of the Icelandic population lived in densely-populated areas ( cities ) , while approximately 21 percent lived in intermediate urbanized areas ( towns and suburbs ) . The share of the population living in thinly populated areas ( rural areas ) decreased slightly in the years from 2012 to 2019 , from 16.7 percent in 2012 to 15.2 percent in 2019 .
