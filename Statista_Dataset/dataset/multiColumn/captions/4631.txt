Until 2018 the money supply in the United Kingdom had been increasing faster than in the United States or Japan , reaching a year-on-year broad money growth rate of 5.3 percent in 2018 . This compares to 4.07 percent in the United States and 2.43 percent in Japan for the same year . However , in 2019 the broad money supply in the United Kingdom declined by -1.34 percent , whereas it grew by almost 8.4 percent in the United States . Broad money is the most inclusive method of calculating the money supply within a given economy . It is likely that the global coronavirus ( COVID-19 ) pandemic will impact the broad money supply across many countries as governments enact stimulus packages in response to the resulting economic downturn .
