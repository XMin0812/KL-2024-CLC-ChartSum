This statistic shows total domestic consumption expenditure in the United Kingdom ( UK ) from 2005 to 2019 , broken down by spending categories . In the period under consideration , household expenditure has remained fairly stable with only slight changes in the share of spend . Alcohol and tobacco , for example , has decreased from five percent in the year 2010 to three percent by 2019 . Household consumption expenditure looks at the overall spending on consumer goods and services of a wide variety . Some examples are government licenses and permits , such as a passport renewal or the price of train tickets to get to work . Housing may also be accounted for in these figures . This figure is measured by how much the consumer actually pays at the point of sale . All fast moving consumer goods such as beer or cigarettes are also accounted for in this data .
