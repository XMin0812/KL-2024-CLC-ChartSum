In U.S. presidential elections since 1964 , voter turnout among male and female voters has changed gradually but significantly since 1964 ; with women consistently voting at a higher rate than men since the 1980 election . 67 percent of eligible female voters took part in the 1964 election , compared to 72 percent of male voters ; this difference has been reversed in recent elections , where the share of female voters in 2016 was larger than the share of male voters by more than four percent .
