Between 2010 and 2013 , there was a noticeable increase in the price of electricity for households in Germany . At the start of the decade households paid less than 24 euro cents per kilowatt-hour . However , this increased by more than five euro cents by the second half of 2013 . In the preceding years prices remained relatively stable , peaking at 30.88 euro cents in the first half of 2019 . By the second half , German households were paying 28.73 euro cents per kilowatt-hour .
