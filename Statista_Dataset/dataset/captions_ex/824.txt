South Korea and China had the highest landed price of liquefied natural gas ( LNG ) in the world at 2.16 U.S. dollars per million Btu , compared with the United States ( Lake Charles ) at 1.70 U.S. dollars per million Btu . LNG landed prices refers to the price that is received at the regasification terminal . These prices are determined by a netback price which is based on the effective price for a seller or producer at a defined location . The global trade volume of LNG has steadily increased over the last few decades .
