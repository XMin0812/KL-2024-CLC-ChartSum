The import value of fuel and lubricants to Kenya decreased significantly in May 2020 , amounting 5.6 billion Kenyan shillings ( KSh ) ( roughly 52 million U.S. dollars ) . The measurement maintained a downward tendency , as the country already registered a severe drop from March to April 2020 . The massive decrease coincides with a period of turbulence in the fuel industry worldwide . In April 2020 , the U.S. oil price crashed into negative for the first time in history , among the coronavirus pandemic .
