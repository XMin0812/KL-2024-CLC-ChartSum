In 2016 , the percentage of informal employment in Latin America and the Caribbean was estimated at an average of 53.1 percent . Among the countries shown in the graph , Bolivia had the highest share of employment informality , amounting to almost 81 percent of the total employed population . This means that around eight every ten workers in Bolivia were informally employed as of 2018 . Uruguay was one of the countries with the lowest share of informal employment in the region , with nearly one quarter of the employed population being informally employed .
