This statistic presents data on the degree of inequality in wealth distribution based on the Gini coefficient in Bolivia between 2000 and 2017 . This coefficient measures the deviation of the distribution of income ( or consumption ) among individuals or households in a given country from a perfectly equal distribution . A value of 0 represents absolute equality , whereas 100 would be the highest possible degree of inequality . As of 2017 , Bolivia had a Gini coefficient of 44 , an improvement from 48.4 as of 2015 .
