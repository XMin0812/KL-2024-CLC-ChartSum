Angola and Niger recorded the highest crude birth rates in Africa in 2018 . In both countries , 44 births were recorded per 1,000 inhabitants , respectively . Mali and Chad followed in the ranking , whereas Uganda 's birth rate equaled to 43 births of every thousand individuals . As of 2018 , half of the population in Angola and Niger was aged under 16 years old . Especially , Niger ranks as the country with the youngest population in Africa and worldwide , with a median age by 15.4 years .
