After the Second World War , the deadliest conflict for the British Armed forces was the Malayan Emergency , which lasted from 1948 to 1960 and resulted in the deaths of 1,442 British soldiers . Over a much longer time period , between 1968 and 2007 , there were 1,441 operational deaths for the British armed forces in Northern Ireland . The Korean War was a noticeably deadly conflict despite its relatively short duration and had the third highest number of fatalities for British troops at 1,129 .
