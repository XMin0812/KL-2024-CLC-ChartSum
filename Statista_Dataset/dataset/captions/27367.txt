The Gini coefficient in Finland fluctuated between 25.7 and 27.4 percent from 2008 to 2018 . In 2018 , the Gini coefficient stood at 27.4 percent , indicating an increase in inequality compared to the previous year . The Gini coefficient measures the distribution of income among individuals or households within an economy . A Gini index of 0 corresponds to perfect income equality , while an index of 100 implies perfect income inequality .
