There were ten fatalities due to terrorist attacks in the European Union in 2019 , the fewest number of terrorist fatalities since 2014 when there were just four . During the provided time period , terrorist attacks were relatively low prior to 2015 , with 2011 a clear outlier due to the July 22 attacks in Norway where a lone right-wing terrorist murdered 77 people in a single attack . The two years with the most deaths due to terrorism are 2015 and 2016 , where a wave of Islamic terrorist attacks inspired and sometimes directed by ISIS swept across Europe .
