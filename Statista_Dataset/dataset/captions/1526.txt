Gross domestic product ( GDP ) per capita in Italy in 2018 reached roughly 34,320 U.S. dollars . When analyzed from the regional perspective of the single regions , the highest GDP per capita could be observed in the Northern areas of the country . Leading the 2018 ranking was the province of Bozen , an autonomous region in the North-East of Italy , where the value of GPD per inhabitant was equal to 47 thousand euros . In Aosta Valley and Lombardy , the second and the third regions with the highest gross domestic product per capita , the values amounted to over 38 thousand euros respectively .
