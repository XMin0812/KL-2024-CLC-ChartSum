As of 2018 , 37.9 percent of households in South Africa were female-headed , which amounted to a total of approximately 6.1 million . Provinces with larger portions of rural areas , such as Eastern Cape ( 46.9 percent ) , Limpopo ( 45.8 percent ) and KwaZulu-Natal ( 45 percent ) , were more likely to share large numbers of female-headed households , whereas rather urbanized provinces , such as Gauteng ( 29.8 percent ) , which includes Johannesburg , and Western Cape ( 32.5 percent ) , which includes Cape Town , were less likely to have female-headed households in comparison .
