In 2019 Russia had the highest incarceration rate in Europe , at 386.1 prisoners per 100 thousand inhabitants , followed by Turkey , which had an incarceration of 329 . The country with the lowest incarceration rate in this year was Lichtenstein , which had 31.3 people in prison for every 100 thousand inhabitants . Germany had one of the lowest rates of 76.7 when compared with other major European countries such as France and England & Wales , which had rates of 104.5 and 137.6 respectively .
