In May 2020 , retail business owners had mostly negative expectations of their financial future and the economic climate in which they would operate in the next three months . A widespread economic forecast survey revealed that the balance of positive versus negative prognoses was -12 percent , indicating that there were more negative than positive responses . This data stands in stark contrast to the survey results of May 2017 , 2018 , and 2019 , when economic outlooks were generally positive . The main reason for the rise of this financial insecurity was the COVID-19 outbreak in the Netherlands , which had devastating effects on the retail industry .
