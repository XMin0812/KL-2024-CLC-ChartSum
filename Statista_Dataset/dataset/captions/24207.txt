In the first half of 2020 , 45 percent of tourism businesses in Kenya estimated a revenue loss above 2.5 million Kenyan shillings , as an effect of the coronavirus ( COVID-19 ) pandemic . Roughly 23 percent indicated loosing from 500 thousand to 1 million KSh , while approximately 15 percent of businesses pointed a loss of revenue above 500 thousand KSh . As of December 5 , 2020 , Kenya had over 87 thousand confirmed cases of coronavirus Measures to contain the outbreak , such as a night curfew and the closing of bars , are still in force . However , under tight COVID-19 regulations , the country resumed international flights on August 1 .
